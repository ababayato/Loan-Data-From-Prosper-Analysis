Loan Data From Prosper Explanatory Analysis¶

By Damola Babayato¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the factors that affect a loan to be granted, the reason why people take loan also what amount is granted to certain borrower based on their status and occupation. Furthermore can the borrower's income influence the loan amount.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others. The data was subseted to 25 data points to aid my analysis.

See this Data Dictionary to understand the dataset's variables.

Distribution of Loan Original Amount¶

The origination amount of the loan in the dataset having the highest loan amount between 3k and 5k with an increase between 10k and 20k. Plotted on a logarithmic scale, the distribution of loan original amount takes on a multimodal shape.

Borrower's Occupation¶

Plotting the borrower's occupations shows a large number of people specified their occupation to be "others". Thereby depicting professionals, computer programmers and executives as the top 3 occupations of loan borrowers.

Employment Status¶

We can see that though the people who are employed take the most loans while people who are unemployed or retired take the least loans.

Income Range Distribution¶

Income range takes on a range of values, from 0 (in dollars) lowest to about 100k (in dollars) at the highest. Plotted a bimodal and skewed right distribution that shows people with income range of $25,000-49,999 are the ones who are taking loans the most.

Loan Amount by Income Range¶

We can see how the higher your income range the higher the chances of getting a high loan amount. We see earners of 50k above can get up to 15k loan amount unlike the people who earn lesser.

BorrowerRate vs LoanOriginalAmount vs LenderYield¶

Plotted on a heatmap, we can see that loans of borrower rate of 0.2 to 0.3 yielded more interest rate on the loan than others.
